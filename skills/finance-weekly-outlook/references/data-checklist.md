# Data Checklist

Use this checklist to plan current-data collection. Load only the parts relevant to the user's market/date.

## Market Behavior

- Price and volume: index/sector ETF/stock weekly return, daily close, relative strength, 20-day/50-day moving averages, volume vs 20-day average.
- Fund flow: US ETF flows if available; A-share northbound/southbound flow if available; main capital flow only as secondary evidence.
- Technical levels: prior high/low, support/resistance, gap, breakout/failure, volume confirmation.
- Breadth: advance/decline ratio, 52-week highs/lows, percentage above 50-day/200-day moving average when available.

## Sentiment and Structure

- US: VIX, put/call ratio, high-yield spread, market breadth, sector rotation.
- A-share: limit-up/limit-down count, 连板高度, turnover, margin financing balance, China volatility indicator if available.
- Crowding: fund positioning, ETF assets/flows, institutional ownership changes, analyst rating concentration, valuation percentile.

## Liquidity

- US: Fed policy signal, US 10Y/2Y yields, DXY, Fed balance sheet, TGA/reverse repo context when relevant.
- China: PBOC operations, China 10Y yield, SHIBOR/DR007, credit impulse proxies, margin financing.
- Cross-border: USD/CNH, northbound flow, commodity sensitivity if relevant.

## Expectations

- Consensus EPS/revenue revisions.
- Earnings calendar, guidance, major order/product/regulatory catalysts.
- Estimate changes after major news.
- "Priced in" proxy: pre-news price move, valuation percentile, crowded positioning, options skew.

## Source Preference

- Official releases and filings first.
- Reuters/Bloomberg/FT/WSJ/财新/第一财经 for news verification.
- Exchange, index provider, ETF issuer, company IR, and central-bank pages for numbers.
- Secondary market-data portals only when primary data is unavailable; label limitations.

## Decision Tests

- Bullish industry: catalyst + market-behavior confirmation + non-crowded or still-underpriced expectation gap.
- Bearish industry: negative catalyst or earnings revision + failed breakout/weak breadth + crowding or valuation vulnerability.
- Bullish stock: industry support + stock-specific catalyst + clear entry/invalidation + manageable crowding.
- Bearish/avoid stock: deteriorating assumptions + weak price confirmation + high expectation or crowded positioning.

Never use a single stock move to infer an industry trend without industry-level confirmation.
