# 证据与推理标准

Use this reference when researching external trends, market demand, roles, or commercial opportunities.

## Source Priority

Prefer sources in this order:

1. Government, regulator, central bank, public statistics bureau, standards bodies.
2. Listed-company filings, annual reports, investor presentations, earnings transcripts.
3. Reputable international organizations, universities, peer-reviewed papers, think tanks.
4. Major consulting or industry reports with transparent methodology.
5. Large recruitment platforms, salary surveys, job posting datasets.
6. Reputable media reports and expert interviews.
7. Startup/product examples, founder posts, social media anecdotes.

Do not use low-priority sources as the only basis for a major recommendation.

## Required Evidence for Claims

Use web research for current or changeable claims:

- Market size, growth rate, funding, salary, job demand, regulation, price levels.
- AI substitution or productivity claims.
- Emerging roles, new business models, technology adoption.
- Current macro conditions and policy direction.

When web access is unavailable, state that external evidence is unverified and downgrade confidence.

## Reasoning Discipline

For every major recommendation, include:

- Premise: what must be true.
- Evidence: what sources support it.
- Inference: why the evidence implies this path fits the person.
- Risk: what could break the conclusion.
- Fallback: what to do if the premise fails.

Example:

| Recommendation | Premise | Evidence needed | If premise fails |
|---|---|---|---|
| Build AI workflow consulting for clinics | Clinics have budget and repetitive admin pain | Healthcare admin cost reports, interviews with clinic operators, job/process data | Shift to back-office automation for private education or local services |

## Avoid These Errors

- Do not infer broad demand from one successful case.
- Do not treat "market is large" as "this person can enter it".
- Do not recommend a startup path without customer access or validation method.
- Do not recommend credentials or degrees without explaining ROI, time cost, and opportunity cost.
- Do not confuse user interest with customer willingness to pay.
- Do not treat AI as either total replacement or pure opportunity; analyze task-level substitution.

## Confidence Labels

Use these labels in the report:

- High confidence: multiple reliable sources and the user's resources fit.
- Medium confidence: reliable macro evidence but personal fit or customer access needs validation.
- Low confidence: plausible but weak evidence, niche market, or unclear payment path.
- Needs validation: must be tested by interviews, landing pages, paid pilots, job applications, or prototype usage.

## Validation Methods

Choose methods that match the path:

- Career transition: 20 job descriptions, 5 informational interviews, 3 portfolio artifacts, 10 targeted applications.
- Consulting/service: 15 customer interviews, 3 paid pilots, a repeatable proposal, documented ROI.
- Product/SaaS: landing page, waitlist, concierge MVP, manual workflow, activation/retention metrics.
- Content/community: 30 posts, 10 expert conversations, conversion to newsletter/community/product.
- Training/course: 5 paid learners, before/after skill proof, repeatable curriculum, referral rate.
